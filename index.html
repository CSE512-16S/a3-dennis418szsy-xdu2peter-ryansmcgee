Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS)
is a potentially fatal airborne viral disease that caused a global epidemic in 2003. SARS first appeared in Canton, China during the winter of 2002. In early 2003, SARS spread worldwide when guests at the Metropark Hotel Kowloon in Hong Kong contracted the virus and travelled abroad. No known transmission has occurred since 2004.

The World Health Organization provides data on the number of incidences of SARS during this outbreak. (Note: Cases resulting in death or recovery were not denoted until April 10, 2003)
